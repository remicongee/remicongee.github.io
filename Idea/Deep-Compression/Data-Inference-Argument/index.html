Data Inference Argument

Posted on 2018-08-25 | In Idea , Deep Compression

Overview

This text summarizes the most common methods in network pruning literature and provides a new view connecting the very methods. The methods discussed below include noise injection, norm-based pruning, Hessian-based pruning and variational dropout.

Noise Injection

The first purpose of pruning is to compress networks, especially deep ones. Moreover, the performance of the network pruned is supposed to be acceptable. Many recent works reveals the redundance of deep networks and argue that simplifying networks in a reasonable way may, by contrast, improve the generalization capacity by reducing the redundance. However, with regard to the generalization capacity, which is explained as the ability of giving similar predictions for similar data inputs, the root problem is that the training data is not enough when compared with millons of model parameters.

Given that, noise injection is proposed. In each training batch, data is multiplied by a noise often sampled from Gaussian distribution so that the diversity of data is raised compared with before. Intuitively, the network sees more data, which is though made artificially, and hence improves prediction quality. This method attempts to solve the problem at the origin, however, the result depends much on the noise distribution, especially the variance. In fact, noise injection includes some effects of regularization, and the variance is the main hyper-parameter controlling the level of this regularization.

  • $W$ becomes sparse after pruned but also not structured.
  • Since weights are considered independently, so should be thresholds. But millions of thresholds are costly.
